Hemanth Neelgund Ramesh

Hi, I am Hemanth (pronounced hey-month). I'm a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Washington, advised by Prof. Shijing Sun. My research focuses on digitalizing battery manufacturing using generative models, and on diffusion models for route optimization problems.

Earlier, I completed my MS in Materials Science and Engineering at UW with Prof. Guozhong Cao and Prof. Shijing Sun, working on battery informatics and explainable machine learning. Before that, I was an undergraduate at NITK Surathkal, advised by Prof. K Narayan Prabhu.

In a past life, I worked on electrolyzer two-phase flow and computer vision projects at Electric Hydrogen in Natick (2023), mentored by Dr. Elif Karatay.

Research Interests

I'm interested in accelerating sustainable battery manufacturing using AI — combining generative models, diffusion models, and explainable machine learning with experimental materials science. Current projects include cost modeling the impact of AI and robotics on lithium metal battery manufacturing (with PNNL), generative AI for battery informatics and predictive modeling (with Amazon), and an AI-robotics-human collaborative platform for sustainable innovations in battery manufacturing.
Cool Stuff I've Done

CIDER — energy-saving routing optimization for electric delivery vans using diffusion models, with Amazon. Poster

EV Range Anxiety — driver behavior-guided battery usage prediction to reduce range anxiety. Code

Battery Manufacturing Cost Modeling — smart manufacturing cost modeling for lithium metal batteries, with PNNL, part of the DOE AMMTO Initiative.

Two-Phase Flow Vision — computer vision models (OpenCV/ImageJ) to characterize two-phase flow in electrolyzers at Electric Hydrogen.

Sodium-Ion Cathodes — investigated K+ pre-intercalated vanadium pentoxide cathodes for improved sodium-ion battery stability and kinetics.
